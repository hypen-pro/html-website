Infected Files

An infected file is a file that has been impacted by a computer virus in any of several ways. Anti-virus technologies work to quarantine an infected file, and may, in some cases, repair the file by removing the virus code. Infected files often come from remote sources through downloads to infect a host computer.

The negative impact of a computer infection


A virus or malware will negatively impact your system, leaving you vulnerable to fraud, but the degree of impact depends on the infection’s type and scope. Here are some of the possible consequences of a computer infection:


1.Loss of performance: Malware can make your computer process things more slowly, hogging system resources and taking far longer than usual to get work done.


2.Loss of bandwidth: Botnets are networks of computers serving a central remote system, often distributing large volumes of data and consuming all broadband bandwidth.


3.Loss of function: In extreme cases, the virus can degrade your computer performance or programming so severely that you can no longer use your machine.
